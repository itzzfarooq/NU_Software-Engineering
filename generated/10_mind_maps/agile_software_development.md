# Agile Software Development — Mind Map

```
Agile Software Development
│
├── 1. WHY AGILE EXISTS
│   ├── Fast-changing business requirements
│   ├── Plan-driven development too slow/rigid
│   ├── Need for rapid delivery & evolution
│   └── Late 1990s reaction to heavyweight methods
│
├── 2. AGILE MANIFESTO (2001)
│   ├── Individuals & interactions  >  Processes & tools
│   ├── Working software           >  Comprehensive documentation
│   ├── Customer collaboration     >  Contract negotiation
│   └── Responding to change       >  Following a plan
│
├── 3. 12 PRINCIPLES
│   ├── Welcome changing requirements
│   ├── Deliver working software frequently
│   ├── Business + developers work daily
│   ├── Motivated individuals, trust them
│   ├── Face-to-face conversation
│   ├── Working software = primary progress measure
│   ├── Sustainable development, constant pace
│   ├── Continuous attention to technical excellence
│   ├── Simplicity (maximize work not done)
│   ├── Self-organizing teams
│   ├── Regular reflection & adjustment
│   └── Customer involvement throughout
│
├── 4. 5 AGILE PRINCIPLES
│   ├── Customer involvement
│   ├── Incremental delivery
│   ├── People not process
│   ├── Embrace change
│   └── Maintain simplicity
│
├── 5. EXTREME PROGRAMMING (XP)
│   ├── XP Release Cycle
│   │   ├── Select user stories
│   │   ├── Break stories into tasks
│   │   ├── Plan release
│   │   ├── Develop/Integrate/Test
│   │   ├── Release software
│   │   └── Evaluate → loop back
│   │
│   ├── 10 XP Practices
│   │   ├── Incremental planning
│   │   ├── Small releases
│   │   ├── Simple design
│   │   ├── Test-first development (TDD)
│   │   ├── Refactoring
│   │   ├── Pair programming
│   │   ├── Collective ownership
│   │   ├── Continuous integration
│   │   ├── Sustainable pace
│   │   └── On-site customer
│   │
│   └── Key Techniques
│       ├── User stories (As a... I want... so that...)
│       ├── Planning game (team estimation)
│       ├── Automated testing (xUnit, JUnit)
│       └── UI prototyping
│
├── 6. SCRUM
│   ├── Scrum Principles
│   │   ├── Controlled chaos
│   │   ├── Commitment
│   │   ├── Information radiator
│   │   ├── Time-boxing
│   │   ├── Emergent requirements
│   │   └── People not process
│   │
│   ├── 3 Roles
│   │   ├── Scrum Master (facilitator, protector)
│   │   ├── Product Owner (customer voice, backlog)
│   │   └── Team (self-organizing, 5-7 people)
│   │
│   ├── 5 Events
│   │   ├── Sprint (2-4 weeks)
│   │   ├── Sprint Planning
│   │   ├── Daily Stand-up (15 min, 3 questions)
│   │   ├── Sprint Review
│   │   └── Sprint Retrospective
│   │
│   ├── 3 Artifacts
│   │   ├── Product Backlog
│   │   ├── Sprint Backlog
│   │   └── Burndown Chart
│   │
│   └── Benefits & Problems
│       ├── Benefits: empowerment, morale, transparency, prioritization
│       └── Problems: role conflict, imposition w/o training, lack of authority
│
├── 7. SCRUM vs XP
│   ├── Scrum = Management framework
│   ├── XP = Technical practices
│   └── COMPLEMENTARY, not alternatives
│
├── 8. AGILE vs TRADITIONAL (17 Differences)
│   ├── Team: Whole team  vs  Hierarchy
│   ├── Requirements: User stories  vs  Analyst-written specs
│   ├── Iteration: 1-4 weeks  vs  2-3 months
│   ├── Acceptance tests: During iteration  vs  At end
│   ├── Programming: Pair programming  vs  Solo + QA
│   ├── Testing: TDD  vs  After coding
│   ├── Ownership: Collective  vs  Manager
│   ├── Integration: Continuous  vs  End of module
│   ├── Pace: Sustainable (marathon)  vs  80/20 sprint
│   ├── Workspace: Open room  vs  Cubicles
│   ├── Design: Simple, just-in-time  vs  Big upfront
│   ├── Refactoring: Continuous  vs  End of project
│   ├── Documentation: Light  vs  Intensive
│   ├── Task allocation: Team self-selects  vs  PM assigns
│   ├── Progress: Burndown chart  vs  Task count
│   ├── Status: Daily stand-up  vs  Weekly PM meeting
│   └── Project status: Status board  vs  Completed tasks
│
├── 9. APPLICABILITY
│   ├── Good for
│   │   ├── Small/medium product development
│   │   ├── Custom systems with committed customer
│   │   └── Non-critical systems
│   │
│   └── NOT suitable for
│       ├── Large systems (difficult to scale)
│       ├── Safety-critical systems (need docs)
│       ├── Outsourced development (contract issues)
│       ├── Complex non-functional requirements
│       └── Organizations with rigid process standards
│
└── 10. SCALING AGILE
    ├── Problems
    │   ├── Large teams can't maintain XP practices
    │   ├── Communication overhead
    │   ├── Legacy/plan-driven components
    │   └── Separate teams conflict with collective ownership
    │
    └── Strategies
        ├── Scale up: Adapt agile for whole system
        │   └── More architecture, docs, formal coordination
        └── Scale out: Agile for components
            └── Plan-driven for system integration
```
